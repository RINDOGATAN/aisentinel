# AI-governance landscape through mid-2026 — research report for AI SENTINEL

**Retrieval date for all sources: 2026-07-17.** Method: parallel search agents + adversarial
verification agents over primary sources where possible (Commission/EDPB/OECD/NIST/ISO;
Consilium/EUR-Lex landing pages sometimes 403'd — noted inline). Confidence tags:
**[P]** primary source fetched · **[S]** secondary (law firm / trade press / vendor) · **[U]** unconfirmed.

**Headline:** The **Digital Omnibus on AI is adopted** (EP 16 Jun 2026; Council 29 Jun 2026;
signed 8 Jul 2026; **not yet in the Official Journal as of 2026-07-17**). It defers Annex III
standalone high-risk obligations from **2 Aug 2026 → 2 Dec 2027** and Annex I embedded
high-risk from **2 Aug 2027 → 2 Aug 2028**, while **keeping 2 Aug 2026 for Art. 50
transparency and the start of Commission GPAI enforcement**, and adds two new Art. 5
prohibitions effective **2 Dec 2026**. This single fact makes AI Sentinel's seeded Art. 113
timeline stale.

## 2. FRIA practice (Art. 27)

- **2.1 Scope + six elements [P].** Deployers that are public bodies / private providers of
  public services, plus Annex III 5(b) credit scoring and 5(c) life/health-insurance pricing;
  elements (a)–(f) incl. period/frequency, affected persons, human-oversight implementation,
  and **complaint mechanisms**; MSA notification of results (Art. 27(3)); FRIA complements a
  DPIA (Art. 27(4)). <https://ai-act-service-desk.ec.europa.eu/en/ai-act/article-27>
- **2.2 The Art. 27(5) AI Office template is NOT published** (verified by refutation attempt);
  with FRIA now effectively biting 2 Dec 2027 there is runway.
- **2.3 APDCAT FRIA model — the benchmark methodology [P, read in full].** Presented 28 Jan
  2025 (Mantelero): 4-section scoping (system/data flows; FR context; existing controls;
  stakeholder + supply-chain due diligence); per-right **Likelihood = probability × exposure,
  Severity = gravity × reversibility effort**, 4×4 matrices, **no aggregation across rights**,
  residual re-scoring; 4 validated Annex III use cases.
  <https://www.dpdenxarxa.cat/pluginfile.php/2468/mod_folder/content/0/FRIA_en_def.pdf>
- **2.4 Other references:** Dutch FRAIA (2021/22)
  <https://www.government.nl/documents/2021/07/31/impact-assessment-fundamental-rights-and-algorithms>;
  ECNL + Danish Institute for Human Rights guide (Dec 2025, deployment decision + public
  reporting) <https://www.humanrights.dk/publications/guide-fundamental-rights-impact-assessments-under-eu-artificial-intelligence-ai-act>;
  CoE HUDERIA methodology (Nov 2024) + first part of the Model/COBRA (Nov 2025).
  Negative findings: **no official AESIA/AEPD FRIA template** — for ES locale cite APDCAT.

## 3. NIST AI RMF ecosystem & ISO/IEC 42001 practice

*(From the dedicated NIST/ISO research thread; all retrievals 2026-07-17.)*

- **AI RMF 1.0 remains current — no 1.1/2.0 exists**, but America's AI Action Plan
  (23 Jul 2025) formally tasks NIST with revising it (removing misinformation/DEI/climate
  references). Any "RMF 1.1" claim is consultancy-blog only [U].
  <https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/Americas-AI-Action-Plan.pdf> ·
  <https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework>
- **Generative AI Profile NIST-AI-600-1 (Jul 2024) unrevised** [P]
  <https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/ai/NIST.AI.600-1.pdf>. New companions instead:
  **Cyber AI Profile IR 8596** preliminary draft 16 Dec 2025
  <https://csrc.nist.gov/pubs/ir/8596/iprd>; **COSAiS** SP 800-53 AI overlays (concept
  14 Aug 2025; first outline Jan 2026) <https://csrc.nist.gov/projects/cosais>;
  **Zero Drafts** pilot (AI TEVV; AI documentation)
  <https://www.nist.gov/artificial-intelligence/ai-research/nists-ai-standards-zero-drafts-pilot-project-accelerate>;
  US AISI renamed **CAISI** (Jun 2025); Dioptra testbed active.
- **ISO/IEC 42001 practice:** ~**350 certificates worldwide by spring 2026 — unofficial
  estimate only** [M]. Confirmed certified: Anthropic (Jan 2025), AWS (Nov 2024, first major
  cloud), Microsoft (Copilot family; M365 Copilot recertified May 2026 zero NCs), Google
  (GCP/Workspace/Gemini). Audit reality: Annex A = **38 controls / 9 objectives**; SoA is the
  first artifact requested; Stage 1 scope/policies/SoA, Stage 2 evidence per control; 3-year
  cycle + annual surveillance. Common NCs: stale risk registers, over-broad AIMS scope, thin
  SoA, missing impact assessments, no model-monitoring reviews.
  <https://www.schellman.com/blog/iso-certifications/iso-42001-certification-processs> ·
  <https://cloudsecurityalliance.org/blog/2025/05/08/iso-42001-lessons-learned-from-auditing-and-implementing-the-framework>
- **Standards pipeline:** **ISO/IEC 42005:2025** (AI impact assessment, May 2025 — complements,
  not replaces, DPIA/FRIA; natural Art. 27 methodology backbone)
  <https://www.iso.org/standard/42005>; **ISO/IEC 42006:2025** (certification-body
  requirements, Jul 2025 — unblocked accreditation) <https://www.iso.org/standard/42006>;
  42007 (DIS, ~2027); **27090 AI security** (FDIS Mar 2026, publication H2 2026)
  <https://www.iso.org/standard/56581.html>; 25059 2nd ed. in DIS.
  **No ISO 42001 amendment of any kind exists**; EU alignment runs via CEN/CENELEC prEN 18286,
  and 42001 is **not** a harmonised standard.
- **Accreditation:** ANAB ~9 US CBs (Nov 2025); BSI first UKAS+RvA CB (Nov 2025), triple
  accreditation Mar 2026; SCC pilot-first. **No published IAF global 42006 transition
  deadline** — do not state one in product copy.
- **Mapping authorities:** NIST-hosted **AI RMF ↔ ISO/IEC 42001 crosswalk**
  <https://airc.nist.gov/docs/NIST_AI_RMF_to_ISO_IEC_42001_Crosswalk.pdf> (strongest citable
  authority); **CSA AI Controls Matrix v1.1** (14 Jul 2026: 247 controls, mappings to EU AI

  <https://cloudsecurityalliance.org/blog/2026/07/14/ai-controls-matrix-v1-1-strengthening-the-foundation-for-trustworthy-ai>.
- **Copy traps:** ~350-cert figure is unofficial; no RMF 1.1 text exists; no 42001 amendment

## 4. AI incident reporting norms

- **4.1 Art. 73 mechanics confirmed [P].** Serious incident = Art. 3(49)(a)–(d); deadlines
  ≤15 days general, ≤2 days widespread infringement/critical infrastructure, ≤10 days death;
  incomplete-then-complete allowed; recipient = MSA of the Member State(s) of occurrence.
  <https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/article/73/> — **AI Sentinel's seeded 15/10/2 ladder
  is correct.**
- **4.2 Art. 73(7) guidance still DRAFT** (draft + reporting template 26 Sep 2025;
  consultation closed 7 Nov 2025; no final)
  <https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/consultations/ai-act-commission-issues-draft-guidance-and-reporting-template-serious-ai-incidents-and-seeks>.
  With Annex III deferred, Art. 73 for high-risk effectively bites **2 Dec 2027**
  (confirmed by implication — no carve-out found).
- **4.3 GPAI systemic-risk incident regime is LIVE [P].** Art. 55 since 2 Aug 2025; Commission
  **GPAI serious-incident template 4 Nov 2025**
  <https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/library/ai-act-commission-publishes-reporting-template-serious-incidents-involving-general-purpose-ai>;
  Code of Practice Commitment 9 ladder (verified from the chapter PDF
  <https://ec.europa.eu/newsroom/dae/redirection/document/118119>): initial report **2 days**
  critical infrastructure, **5 days** serious cybersecurity breach (incl. weight
  exfiltration), **10 days death only**, **15 days** health/fundamental-rights/property;
  intermediate reports ≥ every 4 weeks; final ≤60 days after resolution; records ≥5 years.
- **4.4 OECD [P].** Common reporting framework (AI Papers No. 34, 28 Feb 2025): **29 criteria /
  8 dimensions, 7 mandatory**; AIM renamed "AI Incidents and Hazards Monitor"; **incident =
  actual harm vs hazard = plausible harm**. <https://oecd.ai/en/incidents-methodology>
- **4.5 Taxonomies:** MIT AI Risk Repository (Dec 2025: 1,700+ risks, 7 domains / 24
  subdomains) <https://airisk.mit.edu/blog/repository-update-december-2025>; AIID counts [U].

## 6. Genuinely new developments

- **GPAI enforcement starts 2 Aug 2026** (Art. 91–93 powers, Art. 101 fines to 3 %/€15M;
  pre-Aug-2025 models have until 2 Aug 2027)
  <https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/guidelines-gpai-providers>.
- **GPAI CoP signatories:** 23–24 incl. OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Mistral;
  **xAI Safety&Security chapter only; Meta refused**
  <https://techcrunch.com/2025/07/18/meta-refuses-to-sign-eus-ai-code-of-practice/>.
- **Training-data summaries:** template mandatory since 24 Jul 2025 but only ~5 providers
  published; AI Office verification power from 2 Aug 2026
  <https://aial.ie/research/gpai-training-transparency/>.
- **Member-state readiness patchy:** 9 fully designated MSAs / 12 partial / 6 none (17 Jun
  2026); essentially no notified bodies — a core omnibus rationale
  <https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/national-implementation-plans/>.
- **Art. 4 literacy:** Commission Q&A — documented, context-tailored measures; living
  repository carries **no presumption of compliance**
  <https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/faqs/ai-literacy-questions-answers>.
- **Enforcement signals:** Garante fined Replika **€5M** (19 May 2025); Clothoff blocked;
  OpenAI €15M fine suspended on appeal; CNIL AI recommendations + PANAME audit tooling; AEPD
  agentic-AI guidance (Feb 2026). **Spain's Organic AI Bill** (Council of Ministers 26 May
  2026): unlabeled deepfakes = serious infringement, fines to €35M/7 %, biting with Art. 50 on
  2 Aug 2026
  <https://privacymatters.dlapiper.com/2026/05/spain-government-approves-the-draft-organic-law-on-the-proper-use-and-governance-of-artificial-intelligence/>.
  (AEPD "147 proceedings/€1.75M" [U — do not use].)
- **EDPB:** Opinion 28/2024 (model anonymity case-by-case; LI 3-step test); AI enforcement
  taskforce (Feb 2025); EDPB-EDPS Joint Opinions 1/2026 + 2/2026 on the omnibus; **joint
  EDPB+Commission GDPR–AI Act interplay guidelines expected later in 2026**.
